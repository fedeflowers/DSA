## Explanation of the LeetCode Solution for "Trips and Users"

### 1. Brief Explanation of the Approach

The provided SQL query calculates the cancellation rate of trips requested between the dates '2013-10-01' and '2013-10-03'. It joins two instances of the `users` table (for clients and drivers) to ensure that both participants of the trip are not banned. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of what the query does:

- **SELECT Clause**: 
  - It retrieves the `request_at` date as `Day`.
  - It calculates the `Cancellation Rate`, defined as the ratio of trips that are not completed (`status != 'completed'`) to the total number of trips (`COUNT(*)`), rounded to two decimal places.

- **FROM and JOIN Clauses**:
  - The query operates on the `trips` table, denoted as `t`, and joins it with the `users` table (twice), once for the client (`c`) and once for the driver (`d`).
  - Both joins filter participants to ensure only users who are not banned (`banned = 'No'`) are included.

- **WHERE Clause**:
  - It restricts the results to trips that occurred within the specified date range.

- **GROUP BY Clause**:
  - The results are grouped by the `request_at` date, allowing for the calculation of cancellation rates for each unique date.

# Write your MySQL query statement below

SELECT 
    request_at AS Day,
    ROUND(
        SUM(status != 'completed') / COUNT(*),
        2
    ) AS "Cancellation Rate"
FROM trips t
JOIN users c ON t.client_id = c.users_id AND c.banned = 'No'
JOIN users d ON t.driver_id = d.users_id AND d.banned = 'No'
WHERE request_at BETWEEN '2013-10-01' AND '2013-10-03'
GROUP BY request_at;
